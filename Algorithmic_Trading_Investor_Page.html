Strategy comparison

The two structure-based strategies (blue, purple) risk a fixed 1% of account equity per trade with a hard daily cap. The two hedging/grid strategies (orange, red) size risk in lots and average into baskets — a materially different exposure profile, detailed below.

Visual Comparison

Position exposure by strategy

Maximum concurrent open positions is a useful proxy for exposure scale — showing how many simultaneous trades each strategy can carry at its busiest.

The structure-based and scalping strategies cap exposure at one to three concurrent positions. The two grid/hedging strategies can run into dozens or hundreds of simultaneous positions while averaging into adverse moves — a fundamentally different risk shape worth stress-testing against historical volatility events.
